# Evaluation Methodology

## Benchmark
The benchmark is generated by [`make_dataset.py`](/c:/Users/ANISH%20PC/Desktop/RAG/rag-hallucination-study/make_dataset.py).

It contains:

- 60 total questions
- 40 answerable questions
- 20 unanswerable questions

The questions are constructed against a small local corpus spanning:

- company policy documents
- product manual excerpts
- academic-style article fragments

## Compared Methods

### `keyword`
Retrieves one chunk and answers from that evidence.

Purpose:
- a retrieval-only baseline with minimal grounding logic

### `naive_gpt`
Answers directly without retrieval.

Purpose:
- a baseline for unconstrained LLM behavior

### `rag`
Retrieves top-k evidence chunks, requests citation-grounded output, and refuses when evidence is insufficient.

Purpose:
- the reliability-oriented system under study

## Judging
Per-example scoring is handled by [`evaluate.py`](/c:/Users/ANISH%20PC/Desktop/RAG/rag-hallucination-study/evaluate.py), which calls a structured LLM judge through [`src/llm.py`](/c:/Users/ANISH%20PC/Desktop/RAG/rag-hallucination-study/src/llm.py).

The judge returns:

- `is_correct`
- `has_unsupported_claims`
- `refusal_appropriate`
- `notes`

This is useful because answer correctness and groundedness are not identical:

- an answer can be correct but unsupported by the cited evidence
- an answer can be incorrect even if it contains partial evidence
- a refusal can be the correct behavior on unanswerable questions

## Metrics

### Accuracy on Answerable
Measured over answerable questions only.

Interpretation:
- higher is better
- captures whether the method returns the expected content when evidence exists

### Unsupported Claim Rate
Measured over all examples.

Interpretation:
- lower is better
- captures whether the answer includes content not supported by the available evidence/citations

### Refusal Precision
Among all refusals, how many occurred on truly unanswerable questions.

Interpretation:
- higher is better
- penalizes unnecessary refusals

### Refusal Recall
Among all unanswerable questions, how many were refused.

Interpretation:
- higher is better
- penalizes hallucinated answers on unsupported questions

### Cost and Latency
The experiment also records:

- estimated API cost
- average latency per method

These are secondary metrics, but useful when comparing reliability tradeoffs.

## Interpretation Guidance
For recruiter-facing discussion, the most important story is not raw accuracy alone.

The more important reliability question is:

> Does the system avoid making unsupported claims, especially on unanswerable questions?

That is why refusal behavior and unsupported claim rate are central metrics in this repository.

## Important Caveat
If the experiment is run with `USE_MOCK_LLM=1`, the output reflects deterministic mock behavior rather than real model performance. That mode is appropriate for testing plumbing, schema handling, and reproducibility, but not for making strong claims about grounded generation quality.
